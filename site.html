Courchevel

Courchevel in France was founded as a ski resort in 1946, developed rapidly in the following decades, and in 1992 part of the competitions of the XVI Olympic Games took place here. In the XXI century Courchevel has become one of the most prestigious ski resorts in the world.

Meribel

Meribel is a ski resort in the Tarantaise Valley in the French Alps, located near the town of Moutier and the Vanoise National Park. It is located at an altitude of 1400 m above sea level. The founder of the resort is Peter Lindsay, who was looking for a place for ski slopes that would not be in Austria or Germany, because of the growing Nazi movement there. In 1936 he visited these places for the first time, and in 1938 the first lift was built. During the war, construction was suspended, but three years after the end, construction resumed.
